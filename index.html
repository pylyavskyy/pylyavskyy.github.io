About Me

I am a data scientist and optimisation specialist with a strong foundation in operations research, focused on solving complex, real-world problems through data, algorithms, and structured decision-making since 2017. My work centers on designing scalable solutions that improve efficiency, optimize resource allocation, and support high-impact business decisions.

With a background in Management Science and advanced research experience at Lancaster University, I bring a rigorous analytical approach to practical challenges. I specialise in combinatorial optimization and scheduling problems—areas that are critical in industries such as logistics, transportation, workforce planning, and operations management. My experience includes developing and implementing models that handle large-scale constraints, and competing objectives, delivering solutions that are both robust and applicable in real-world settings.

I also place a strong emphasis on communication and impact. I am comfortable translating complex analytical work into clear insights for stakeholders, helping bridge the gap between technical teams and business decision-makers. I believe that the true value of data science lies not only in building models, but in enabling better decisions and tangible improvements in performance.

I am driven by continuous improvement and a curiosity to explore new tools, technologies, and methodologies. I actively seek out challenging problems that require both analytical depth and practical thinking, and I am motivated by delivering solutions that make a meaningful difference.

Ultimately, I aim to work on problems where data, optimisation, and intelligent systems can significantly improve how organizations operate—whether by reducing costs, increasing efficiency, or enabling smarter, faster decisions at scale.
